Beginner's Guide


Basic Supplies

  • Crochet Hooks: Start with basic set of hooks in various sizes to accomodate different yarn weights.
  • Yarn: Choose a yarn in a color and weight that appeals to you. For beginners, medium-weight yarn is a good place to start.
  • Scissors: A sharp pair of scissors will come in handy for cutting yarn.
  • Stitch Markers: Couple of stitch markers to keep track of the beginning of new rows.

Basic Crochet Stitches

  • Chain Stitch: Foundational stitch for most crochet projects.
  • Single Crochet: Creates a dense and sturdy fabric.
  • Double Crochet: Taller than single crochet, great for creating texture.

Common Abbreviations

  • ch: Chain
  • sc: Single Crochet
  • dc: Double Crochet
  • inc: Increase (work 2 stitches into one stitch)
  • dec: Decrease (work 2 stitches together)
Familiarise yourself with the abbreviations to understand pattern instructions.

First Projects

  • Begin with a simple project to practice your newfound skills. A beginner-friendly project could be a dishcloth, scarf, or simple granny square.
  • Follow along with beginner-friendly patterns found in the video linked below.
  • Peruse through the web for simple and easy patterns.

"To err is human, to forgive divine."

  • Don't be discouraged by mistakes!
    Crocheting is a learning process, and everyone makes errors along the way. If you encounter difficulties, consult online tutorials or ask for help from experienced crocheters in online communities.

Practice!

  • Like any skill, crochet improves with practice.
    Don't rush through projects—enjoy the process and take pride in your creations, no matter how simple they may seem.
